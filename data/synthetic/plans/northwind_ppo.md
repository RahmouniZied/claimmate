§1.1 Definitions
"Medically necessary" means health care services that a prudent provider would provide to diagnose or treat an illness or injury, consistent with generally accepted standards of medical practice and not primarily for convenience.

§4.1 Outpatient Services
Covered outpatient services include office visits, laboratory tests, and diagnostic procedures ordered by a treating provider.

§4.2 Advanced Diagnostic Imaging
Advanced imaging, including MRI, CT, and PET, is covered when ordered by a treating provider and the medical necessity criteria in §4.3 are met. Prior authorization is required, except for imaging performed during an emergency admission.

§4.3 Medical Necessity Criteria for Imaging
Advanced imaging of the spine is considered medically necessary when the member has completed at least six weeks of conservative treatment, OR when red-flag symptoms such as progressive neurologic deficit, suspected malignancy, or trauma are documented. Documentation of either condition satisfies this section.

§5.1 Emergency Services
Emergency services are covered under the prudent layperson standard. Coverage applies when a prudent layperson, acting reasonably, would have believed that an emergency medical condition existed, regardless of the final diagnosis. Prior authorization is not required for emergency services.

§5.2 Post-Stabilization Care
Care provided after the member is stabilized following an emergency is covered when the plan is notified within 24 hours or as soon as reasonably possible.

§6.4 Prescription Drug Formulary Exceptions
A drug that is not on the formulary is covered when the prescriber submits a formulary exception request documenting that formulary alternatives have been tried and were ineffective, or are expected to be ineffective or harmful for the member.

§7.2 Appeals
A member may file an internal appeal within 180 days of the date of an adverse benefit determination. Expedited appeals are available for urgent situations.

§9.3 Exclusions: Cosmetic Services
Services performed primarily to improve appearance, and that do not restore bodily function, are excluded from coverage. Reconstructive procedures that restore a bodily function impaired by illness or injury are not excluded under this section.
